APOLO — NITSA

A spatial intervention designed for the legendary Nitsa club in Barcelona. The project reimagines the relationship between sound, light, and architectural geometry through a series of synchronized LED installations that respond to live audio frequencies in real-time.

The visual language focuses on the concept of 'digital structuralism'—using light as a physical material to define and distort the perceived dimensions of the dance floor. By stripping away color and focusing on monochromatic intensity, the installation creates a clinical, focused atmosphere.
